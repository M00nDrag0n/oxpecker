The Red-Billed Oxpecker

The Red-Billed Oxpecker is a bird found in Africa, which is specially designed for cleaning wounds and removing parasites from the bodies of African mammals. These little birds are very useful for preventing widespread infection in large mammals.

Oxpeckers have strong feet that allow them to grip the fur of African mammals with ease. Their beaks have an internal comb-like structure which allows them to file though fur to pick out bugs, rotting skin, and other things they find. Red-billed oxpeckers nest in tree holes which they fill with collected hairs from various mammals that they debug. The only 2 species of oxpecker are red-billed oxpeckers and yellow-billed oxpeckers. As a result of the excessive hunting of buffalos and rhinos, which were their main hosts, as well as arsenic poisoning, oxpeckers almost went extinct and had to be bred in captivity. Currently they are listed as "vulnerable".

Oxpeckers are not tolerated by every animal, such as elephants, waterbucks, and hartebeests.
